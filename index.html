Bulgogi 불고기

11.April 2020

Literally speaking, bulgogi (불고기) means “fire meat”. It is often made with tender parts of beef, pork or chicken. However, in general terms, unless specified, bulgogi is made with beef.
Undoubtedly it is one of the most well-known Korean traditional foods and it has been around for thousands of years.
Naturally, the cooking method of bulgogi evolved over time and it varies by region too. However, to narrow our focus down, we will be talking about the most common way of making beef bulgogi in modern times.

Jajangmyeon 자장면

1.April 2020

Have you ever tried Korean black bean sauce noodles (Jajangmyeon, 자장면 or Jjajangmyeon, 짜장면) before?

It is a popular noodle dish mixed in with black bean sauce, which consists of chunjang (춘장, a salty black soybean paste),
diced pork (or other kinds of meat) and vegetables. You can find it easily at a Korean Chinese restaurant.

While there are many theories about when it was first introduced to Korea, officially the first known Jajangmyeon is from a restaurant called Gonghwachun (공화춘) in Chinatown of Incheon,
Korea back in 1905. It was introduced by Chinese merchants but the flavor evolved to suit Korean’s taste buds over time.
